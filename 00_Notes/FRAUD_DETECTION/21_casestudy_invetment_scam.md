# 📊 Case Study: Vizag Cryptocurrency Investment Scam

## Overview
In late 2025, a couple from Visakhapatnam (Vizag), India, lost approximately **₹1.5 crore (~$180,000)** after being targeted by a fraudulent cryptocurrency investment platform.

The scam used a fake trading system designed to mimic legitimate crypto investment services.

---

# 📍 Timeline of Events

## 1. Initial Contact 🌐
- Victims were approached via social media and links.
- They were introduced to a fake crypto trading platform.
- Scammers helped them create accounts and login credentials.

---

## 2. Small Investments & Fake Profits 📈
- Victims were encouraged to invest small amounts first.
- The platform showed:
  - Fake profits
  - Successful withdrawals
- These “wins” built trust and reduced suspicion.

---

## 3. Large Financial Transfers 💸
- Victims invested:
  - ₹75 lakh
  - ₹69 lakh
- Funds were sent to multiple bank accounts linked to scammers.

---

## 4. Withdrawal Block 🚫
- Victims tried to withdraw large profits.
- Platform denied withdrawal requests.

---

## 5. “Security Fee” Trap ⚠️
- Scam demanded **30% “risk assessment fee”**
- Claimed payment was required to unlock funds
- Victims could not pay additional amount

---

## 6. Disappearance 🕳️
- Platform changed domain or went offline
- Communication stopped completely
- Funds were permanently lost

---

# 🔎 Why This Scam Worked

## 1. Trust Building Through Fake Success 📊
- Small profits and withdrawals created false legitimacy
- Victims believed the system was real

---

## 2. Professional-Looking Platform 🖥️
- Fake dashboards showed balances and profits
- Visual credibility increased trust

---

## 3. Gradual Financial Escalation 📉➡️📈
- Started small → increased gradually
- Reduced suspicion over time

---

## 4. Withdrawal Blocking + Fee Demand 🔐
- Funds became “locked”
- Additional payment demanded to release money
- Classic scam entrapment tactic

---

# 📌 Why Authorities Classify It as Investment Fraud

Authorities identified this as cyber investment fraud because:

- Fake trading platform simulated real crypto exchanges
- Artificial profit dashboards were used
- Controlled withdrawals were used to build trust
- Final goal was financial extraction through escalation

---

# 🌍 Unique Elements of This Case

- Early **withdrawal success illusion**
- Highly structured staged investment model
- Psychological manipulation using confirmation bias
- Domain switching after fraud completion

---

# 🐾 Police & Public Response

## Actions Taken
- FIR registered under cybercrime laws
- Investigation launched against fraud network

## Public Warning
- Avoid unsolicited investment offers
- Do not trust social media trading links
- Use only verified and regulated platforms

## Safety Advice
- Always perform due diligence before investing
- Verify licenses and regulatory status
- Never pay “unlock fees” for investments

---

# 🎯 Key Learning Points

- Fake crypto platforms often simulate real profits
- Early success is used as a manipulation tool
- Gradual escalation increases financial damage
- “Security fee” requests are major fraud indicators
- Disappearing platforms confirm investment scams

---

# 📌 Final Insight

> Fraud succeeds when trust is built through controlled illusion, not reality.

Always verify platforms, not profits.
