# Who Fraudsters Target and Why

Fraudsters carefully choose their targets based on opportunity, access, and vulnerability. Their attacks are usually strategic rather than random, focusing on people or organizations that can provide financial gain, valuable information, or easy manipulation.

---

# Primary Target Groups

## 👤 Individuals
- People with online accounts, digital payment methods, or disposable income are common targets.
- Fraudsters exploit:
  - Lack of awareness
  - Emotional reactions
  - Unsafe online habits
  - Oversharing personal information

## 🏢 Employees in Organizations
- Employees often have access to:
  - Financial systems
  - Sensitive company data
  - Approval processes
- Fraudsters may impersonate managers, coworkers, or vendors to pressure employees into bypassing security checks.

## 👵 Elderly or Less Tech-Savvy Individuals
- Frequently targeted through:
  - Investment scams
  - Phishing attacks
  - Confidence fraud schemes
- Limited knowledge of digital security can increase vulnerability.

## 👩‍🎓 Students and Young Adults
- Often targeted through:
  - Social media scams
  - Online marketplace fraud
  - “Get rich quick” schemes
- Curiosity, peer influence, and limited financial experience increase risk.

---

# Why Fraudsters Choose Specific Targets

Fraudsters focus more on opportunity than intelligence.

### 🔑 Access to Money or Assets
- Executives and employees with financial authority are high-value targets.

### 🧾 Access to Sensitive Information
- Customer data, login credentials, and company records can be sold or exploited.

### ⏳ Vulnerability to Stress or Urgency
- People under pressure are more likely to act quickly without verification.

### 🎯 Predictable Habits and Routines
- Regular activities such as invoice approvals or recurring purchases are easier to exploit.

Fraudsters study behavior patterns to identify moments when trust, emotion, or routine decision-making can be manipulated.

---

# Psychological Tactics Used by Fraudsters

Fraud often relies more on psychology than technical skill.

## ⚠️ Common Psychological Triggers

### Urgency
- Messages demanding immediate action encourage rushed decisions.

### Authority
- Fraudsters pretend to be executives, banks, or official organizations to gain compliance.

### Reciprocity
- Offering rewards or favors creates a feeling of obligation.

### Social Proof
- Fake reviews, trends, or peer activity are used to build trust and credibility.

Understanding these tactics helps individuals recognize suspicious behavior and respond more cautiously.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Fraudsters target individuals, employees, elderly populations, students, and organizations based on access and vulnerability.
- Opportunity and behavioral susceptibility matter more than intelligence.
- Psychological manipulation such as urgency, authority, and social proof is commonly used.
- Awareness of fraud tactics helps reduce risk and improve prevention strategies.
