## Advanced credentials; require significant experience/study

### 🎖️ Advanced Professional Path

**Note:** Professional certifications (CISSP, CISM, CCSK) require multiple years of hands-on experience beyond course study. Below are Coursera preparations.

#### CISSP Preparation (Certified Information Systems Security Professional)
**ISC2 Credential:** The most respected CISSP is NOT directly offered on Coursera but prerequisites can be met through:
1. IBM & ISC2 Cybersecurity Specialist (CC path)
2. SSCP specialization (advanced experience)
3. Official ISC2 study materials (external to Coursera)

**Requirements:** 
- Pass CISSP exam
- 5 years experience (or equivalent with education)
- 8 domains of ISC2 Common Body of Knowledge (CBK)

## CAREER TIMELINE

### Year 1: Entry-Level ($50,000-$70,000 USD)
**Month 1-3:** Complete beginner specialization (Google or IBM)
**Month 4-6:** Earn ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)
**Target Job:** SOC Analyst, Security Analyst, Cybersecurity Associate

### Year 2: Intermediate-Advanced ($70,000-$95,000 USD)
**Month 7-12:** Complete CEH OR SSCP

## LEARNING TIPS

### 1. **Build Portfolio Projects**
- Create GitHub repositories for each specialization
- Implement tools from courses (Python scripts, vulnerability scanners)
- Document projects thoroughly
- Publish on GitHub with detailed READMEs

### 2. **Hands-On Labs**
- Use VirtualBox + Kali Linux for practical work
- Practice on HackTheBox, TryHackMe, OverTheWire
- Set up home lab environment
- Document all explorations

### 3. **Maintain Momentum**
- Commit 2-3 hours/day consistently
- Complete specializations in 3-4 months sequentially
- Don't skip labs/projects for faster completion
- Engage with course forums and peers

### 4. **Certification Strategy**
- Plan exam dates 1-2 months after course completion
- Use official ISC2/EC-Council study materials
- Take practice exams multiple times
- Join study groups

### 5. **Networking**
- Join cybersecurity communities (local meetups, online)
- Connect with course instructors and peers on LinkedIn
- Attend cybersecurity conferences/webinars
- Document learning journey on LinkedIn

### 6. **Stay Current**
- Follow cybersecurity news (Bleeping Computer, Krebs)
- Join professional organizations (ISC2, EC-Council, SANS)
- Subscribe to security newsletters
- Participate in bug bounty programs (HackerOne, Bugcrowd)

### 7. **Time Management**
- Beginner specialization: 3-4 months @ 5-8 hrs/week
- Intermediate specialization: 4-6 months @ 8-10 hrs/week
- Advanced specialization: 4-5 months @ 10-12 hrs/week
- Certification exam prep: 1-2 months @ 15-20 hrs/week

### 8. **Cost Optimization**
- Use Coursera Plus ($239/year) for significant savings
- Many courses offer free audit option
- ISC2 offers free certifications to military/veterans
- Look for employer tuition reimbursement
